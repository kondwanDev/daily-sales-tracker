# User Workflow

## Overview

This document describes the current process used by the shop operator to sell products, record sales, and balance daily sales.

The workflow is based on the current manual process where sales are recorded on paper.

---

# Current Manual Workflow

## 1. Customer Purchase

1. A customer enters the shop.
2. The customer selects one or more products.
3. The customer and shop operator agree on the final selling price.
4. The customer pays for the products.

---

## 2. Recording the Sale

After completing the sale, the shop operator records the transaction manually.

The information recorded includes:

* Product sold.
* Selling price.
* Other necessary sale details.

The process is repeated for every customer throughout the day.

---

## 3. End-of-Day Balancing

At the end of the day, the shop operator:

1. Reviews all recorded sales.
2. Adds all sales amounts manually.
3. Calculates the total money expected from sales.
4. Compares the calculated amount with the cash available.

---

## 4. Owner Verification

After the daily balancing is completed:

1. The shop owner reviews the sales information.
2. The owner verifies the reported sales data.

---

# Problems Identified in Current Workflow

The current process has several challenges:

* Manual calculation takes time.
* Mistakes can occur when recording products.
* Mistakes can occur when recording prices.
* Searching previous sales records is difficult.
* Sales information is not immediately available.
* Business performance is difficult to analyze.

---
